The Nature of Nature

We are the meaning makers
but she — the key — is spiraled onto
herself, the same the same. Uniqueness
yet permeates as needle light in the pines.

Despite the ice, despite the void, despite
the sparse existence of fists in the night;
light like a blood migrates unto the limit
of her body. Any handful holds both
the largest and smallest universe
the same, the same.

Words careful or loose are naught
but staccato — the cold movements
of the frost bitten. Brayed in the moonless
we repeat our howls. Cast our questions

like stones into the tide. But we
are the meaning makers,
the purring of bobcats spools
out of the bark and river rock
yet despite her embrace, despite the constant
Hum, despite the ledger left in snowfall,
we grind our teeth as an axe
hammered.

If you find the quiet ones
if you meet him, the squirrel, he
speaks the fractal aphorisms
of the infinite. She sings through
her emissaries. She severs heads,
bears blossoms and ice. Limitless compassion,
her bosom nurtures new life
from the children of the fallen.

Ego

Given to me a knife and the woods
I waded out to meet the bear.
For years, ever since I heard
the sound she has been making

a ceaseless cacophony; so pervasive it seeps
into the paintings into the poetry of the village.
It is a horrendous yet seductive sound.
A screaming mew, vacillating like a reed.

I found her rubbing and rubbing
up |- against the tree. The vibration
sprayed my toes and I bit my tongue.
When I approached with nothing but a smile

she became irascible and snappish.
Entangled in the strength of her
branches the knife only makes her furious.
Watch her with steadied gaze,

The bear is faster than you
think. It is easier to lull her
to sleep, than pin her on her back.
Cold patience and heart are needed.

At night up |- against the tree,

You might just see her dark eyes
vanish, breast heave, breath deepen.

SKETCH OF A FOREST FALL

We walk amongst the pines,
light peels from the needle nets,

cast upon the asphalt is sunlight.
I inhale the sharp of pine and blood-bark.

Your pupil black, refracts the cast and
sway of fine conifers. I see the code

compile behind your tinted
screens, the registers of red wood

and elevation reading recursively.
The Stellar's Jay lectures on the

lean of the forest. When it sets
its wings to the west

you press against the fence,
and look to find the bird-mind.

We spoke little that day in the forest
falls. We trod the tepid water-paths,
watched them well and drain.

We knew before we parked
our car, before even the blues blasting,
the coming cacophony of our solitude.

Remember when we walked
the paths as penitents hands
pressed like pieces of rice paper?
